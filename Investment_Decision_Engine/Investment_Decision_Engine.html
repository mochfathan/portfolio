Methodological Framework & Academic References

Graham Number & Margin of Safety

Benjamin Graham, Security Analysis (1934) & The Intelligent Investor (1949). The Graham Number defines a defensible maximum price based on earnings power and net asset value. MoS = (GN − Price) / Price.

GN = √(22.5 × EPS_diluted × BVPS)

Piotroski F-Score

Joseph Piotroski, "Value Investing: The Use of Historical Financial Statement Information to Separate Winners from Losers," Journal of Accounting Research (2000). Binary 9-point score across profitability (F1–F4), leverage/liquidity (F5–F6), and operating efficiency (F7–F9).

F = ROA + ΔROA + OCF + Accruals + ΔLevr + ΔLiquid + NoDilution + ΔGM + ΔAT

Altman Z-Score

Edward Altman (1968). Public company model using five financial ratios. Z > 2.99: Safe · 1.81–2.99: Grey · < 1.81: Distress. Note: X4 component uses Market Cap; results for private firms may require substituting Market Cap with Book Value.

Z = 1.2X₁ + 1.4X₂ + 3.3X₃ + 0.6X₄ + 1.0X₅

Magic Formula (Greenblatt)

Joel Greenblatt, The Little Book That Beats the Market (2006). Simultaneous ranking on Earnings Yield (EBIT/EV) and Return on Invested Capital (EBIT/IC). Higher rank on both dimensions = strongest candidates.

EY = EBIT/EV · ROIC = NOPAT/(NWC + Net PPE)

PEG Ratio (Lynch)

Peter Lynch, One Up on Wall Street (1989). Adjusts P/E for earnings growth rate; popularised GARP (Growth at a Reasonable Price) investing. PEG < 1.0: potentially undervalued relative to growth; > 2.0: growth priced in.

PEG = (P/E Ratio) ÷ EPS_Growth_Rate (%)

DCF — Perpetuity Growth Model

Damodaran (2012), Investment Valuation; Brealey, Myers & Allen, Principles of Corporate Finance. 5-year FCF projection discounted at WACC + terminal value via Gordon Growth Model. Intrinsic value = Equity Value ÷ Shares Outstanding.

IV = Σ FCFₜ/(1+r)ᵗ + [FCF₅(1+g_T)/(r−g_T)]/(1+r)⁵ − Net Debt

Price / Sales Ratio

Kenneth Fisher, Super Stocks (1984). One of the first models to use P/S as a primary valuation metric for identifying undervalued "super stocks" before they show significant earnings. Fisher preferred P/S < 0.75.

P/S = Market Cap / Total Revenue
