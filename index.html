About Me

I am an accounting student based in Jakarta with internship experience in finance and tax at IDC Indonesia. My technical interests span FX trading (using the Supply & Demand methodology), quantitative research, and building AI-augmented financial tools.

The projects in this portfolio represent a deliberate strategy: rather than competing with thousands of generic AI developers, I build tools that require both engineering capability and professional domain expertise. PAJAKPINTAR knows Indonesian PPN and PPh 23 law because I learned it during my internship. TRADECRAFT enforces Supply and Demand methodology because I hold the certification. INVESTMENT ENGINE implements Graham, Piotroski, and Greenblatt models because I study them as part of my finance curriculum.

My career trajectory targets data analyst, quantitative analyst, or AI integration specialist roles in Indonesian fintech, banking, or consulting — sectors where domain expertise has historically been the barrier to AI adoption. I am building the portfolio now to demonstrate that the barrier has been crossed.
